Irreducible polynomials in \(\mathbb{F}_p[x]\)

Problem

Let \(p \in \mathbb{F}_p[x]\) be monic and irreducible. Show that \(\exists n \in \mathbb{N}: p \mid x^n - 1\).

Solution

Let \(p \in \mathbb{F}_p[x]\) be monic and irreducible. Construct the companion matrix \(C_p \in \mathbb{F}_p^{\operatorname{grad}(p) \times \operatorname{grad}(p)}\). We have

\[ \operatorname{chpol}_{C_p} = \operatorname{minpol}_{C_p} = p. \]

As \(p\) is irreducible, we have \(p(0) \not= 0\) and therefore \(C_p \in \operatorname{GL}_{\operatorname{grad}(p)}(\mathbb{F}_p)\). As \(\operatorname{GL}_{\operatorname{grad}(p)}(\mathbb{F}_p)\) is a finite group, the subgroup \(\langle C_p \rangle\) is also finite and there exists a \(k \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(C_p^k = 1\), implying that \(p = \operatorname{minpol}_{C_p} \mid x^k - 1\).
