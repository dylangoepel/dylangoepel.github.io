The nullspace of an adjoint map

Exercise

Let \(V\) and \(W\) be finite dimensional unitary spaces. Let \(\alpha \in \operatorname{Hom}(V, W)\). Show that \(\ker(\alpha^*) = (\operatorname{im}(\alpha))^\bot\).

Solution

Let \(w \in \ker(\alpha^*) \setminus \{0_V\}\). For all \(v \in V\), it follows that

\[ 0 = v \cdot \alpha^*(w) = \alpha(v) \cdot w \]

and therefore

\[ w \in (\alpha(v))^\bot \]

and as \(v \in V\) was arbitrary

\[ w \in \operatorname{im}(\alpha)^\bot. \]

Now let \(w \in \operatorname{im}(\alpha)^\bot\). For all \(v \in V\) we have

\[ 0 = \alpha(v) \cdot w = v \cdot \alpha^*(w) \]

and therefore \(\alpha^*(w) \in V^\bot = \{0\} \Rightarrow w \in \ker(\alpha^*)\).
