When is a bilinear form positive definite?

Exercise

Let \(b_\mathcal{A}\) be a bilinear form with \( \mathcal{A} = \begin{pmatrix} \lambda & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 2\lambda & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & \lambda \end{pmatrix} \in \operatorname{Mat}_\mathbb{R}(3, 3) \). Find the set of all \(\lambda \in \mathbb{R}\) for which \(b_\mathcal{A}\) is positive definite.

Solution

As we know, a bilinear form is positive definite iff its Gram matrix has only positive eigenvalues.

\[\begin{aligned} \operatorname{chpol}_\mathcal{A} &= \det \begin{pmatrix} x - \lambda & -1 & 0 \\ -1 & x - 2\lambda & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & x - \lambda \end{pmatrix} \\ &= (x - \lambda)(x - 2\lambda)(x - \lambda) - (x - \lambda) + \lambda \\ &= x^3 + (-\lambda - 2\lambda - \lambda) x^2 + (2\lambda^2 + \lambda^2 + 2\lambda^2)x - 2\lambda^3 - x + \lambda + \lambda \\ &= x^3 - 4\lambda x^2 + (5\lambda^2 - 1) x - 2\lambda^3 + 2\lambda \\ &= (x - 2\lambda)(x - \lambda - 1)(x + 1 - \lambda) \end{aligned}\] \[ \sigma(\mathcal{A}) = \{ 2\lambda, \lambda + 1, \lambda - 1 \} \]

All of these are positive iff \(\lambda > 1\). Therefore, the answer is \((1, +\infty)\).
