A self-adjoint homomorphism

Exercise

Let \(V\) be a euclidean vector space and \(v_0 \in V, \lambda \in \mathbb{R}\). Let \(\alpha : V \to V\) be defined by \(\alpha(v) := \lambda v - (v \cdot v_0) v_0\) for all \(v \in V\). Show that \(\alpha\) is a self-adjoint homomorphism. Find all \(\lambda \in \mathbb{R}\) such that \(\forall v \in V: \alpha(v) \cdot v \geq 0\).

Solution

Let \(x, y \in V\). We have

\[\begin{aligned} \alpha(x) \cdot y &= (\lambda x - (x \cdot v_0) v_0) \cdot y \\ &= (\lambda x) \cdot y - (x \cdot v_0) (v_0 \cdot y) \\ &= x \cdot (\lambda y) - (x \cdot v_0) (y \cdot v_0) \\ &= x \cdot (\lambda y) - x \cdot ((y \cdot v_0) v_0) \\ &= x \cdot (\lambda y - (y \cdot v_0) v_0) \\ &= x \cdot \alpha(y) \end{aligned}\]

and therefore

\[ \alpha^* = \alpha. \]

For \(v \in V\)

\[\begin{aligned} \alpha(v) \cdot v &= (\lambda v - (v \cdot v_0) v_0) \cdot v \\ &= \lambda (v \cdot v) - (v \cdot v_0)^2. \end{aligned}\]

By the Cauchy-Schwartz-inequality, we have

\[\begin{aligned} (v \cdot v_0)^2 \leq (v \cdot v) (v_0 \cdot v_0) \end{aligned}\]

which means that for \(\lambda \geq \| v_0 \|^2\) and \(v \in V\)

\[ \alpha(v) \cdot v \geq 0. \]

As

\[ 0 \leq \alpha(v_0) \cdot v_0 = \lambda (v_0 \cdot v_0) - (v_0 \cdot v_0)^2 \]

implies that \(\lambda \geq (v_0 \cdot v_0)\), the answer is exactly \((\| v_0 \|^2, +\infty)\).
