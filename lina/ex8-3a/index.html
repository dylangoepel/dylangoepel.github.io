On the similarity of finite-dimensional reflections

Exercise

Let \(V\) be a finite dimensional euclidean vector space. Let \(\alpha_1, \alpha_2\) be reflections in \(V\). Show that \(\exists \beta \in O(V): \alpha_1 = \beta^{-1} \circ \alpha_2 \circ \beta\).

Solution

Let \(B = (B_1, \ldots, B_n)\), \(B' = (B_1', \ldots, B_n')\) be orthonormal bases of \(V\) such that

\[\begin{aligned} {_B}(\alpha_1){_B} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & & & \\ & 1 & & \\\ & & \ddots & \\ & & & 1 \end{pmatrix} = {_{B'}}(\alpha_2){_{B'}}. \end{aligned}\]

Now choose \(\beta \in O(V)\) with \({_B}(\beta){_B} = {_B}(\operatorname{id}){_{B'}}\). We have

\[\begin{aligned} {_B}(\beta^{-1} \circ \alpha_2 \circ \beta){_B} &= {_B}(\beta^{-1}){_B} \cdot {_B}(\alpha_2){_B} \cdot {_B}(\beta){_B} \\ &= {_{B'}}(\operatorname{id}){_B} \cdot {_B}(\alpha_2){_B} \cdot {_B}(\operatorname{id}){_{B'}} \\\ &= {_{B'}}(\operatorname{id}){_B} \cdot {_B}(\operatorname{id}){_{B'}} \cdot {_{B'}}(\alpha_2){_{B'}} \cdot {_{B'}}(\operatorname{id}){_B} \cdot {_{B}}(\operatorname{id}){_{B'}} \\ &= {_{B'}}(\alpha_2){_{B'}} \\ &= {_B}(\alpha_1){_B} \end{aligned}\]

and therefore

\[\begin{aligned} \beta^{-1} \circ \alpha_2 \circ \beta = \alpha_1. \end{aligned}\]
