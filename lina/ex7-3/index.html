On the Eigenvalues of Anti-Self-Adjoint Maps

Exercise

Let \(V\) be a finite dimensional unitary space and \(\alpha \in \operatorname{Hom}(V, V)\) anti-self-adjoint. Show that \(\alpha - \operatorname{id}_V\) is an isomorphism and that \((\alpha - \operatorname{id}_V)^{-1} \circ (\alpha + \operatorname{id}_V)\) is unitary.

Solution

As an anti-self-adjoint map, \(\alpha\) is normal and therefore similar to a diagonal matrix with only imaginary diagonal elements, impliying that \(\sigma(\alpha) \subseteq \mathbb{R} i\). We have

\[ \dim \ker(\alpha - \operatorname{id}_V) = \dim E_\alpha(1) = \dim \{0\} = 0. \]

As \(\alpha\) is an injective endomorphism, we can deduce that it is an isomorphism.

As \(\alpha\) is anti-self-adjoint, there exists an orthonormal basis \(B\) of \(V\) such that

\[ {_B}(\alpha){_B} = \begin{pmatrix} ia_1 & & \\ & \ddots & \\ & & ia_n \end{pmatrix}. \]

We have

\[\begin{aligned} {_B}((\alpha - \operatorname{id}_V)^{-1} \circ (\alpha + \operatorname{id}_V)){_B} &= {_B}((\alpha - \operatorname{id}_V)^{-1}){_B} \cdot {_B}(\alpha + \operatorname{id}_V){_B} \\ &= \begin{pmatrix} ia_1 - 1 & & \\ & \ddots & \\ & & ia_n - 1 \end{pmatrix}^{-1} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} ia_1 + 1 & & \\ & \ddots & \\ & & ia_n + 1 \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \begin{pmatrix} \frac{ia_1 + 1}{ia_1 - 1} & & \\ & \ddots & \\ & & \frac{ia_n + 1}{ia_n - 1} \end{pmatrix}. \end{aligned}\]

As

\[ \left| \frac{ia_k + 1}{ia_k - 1} \right| = \frac{| ia_k + 1 |}{| ia_k - 1 |} = \frac{\sqrt{1 + a_k^2}}{\sqrt{1 + a_k^2}} = 1 \]

the matrix \({_B}((\alpha - \operatorname{id}_V)^{-1} \circ (\alpha + \operatorname{id}_V)){_B}\) and therefore the linear map \((\alpha - \operatorname{id}_V)^{-1} \circ (\alpha + \operatorname{id}_V)\) are orthogonal.
