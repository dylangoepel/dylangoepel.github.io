On the Elements of Finite Order of \(O(2)\)

Exercise

A group element \(g \in G\) is said to be of finite order if \(\exists n \in \mathbb{N}: g^n = e\). Find all elements of finite order in \(O(2)\).

Solution

According to the classification of two-dimensional orthogonal maps, \(O(2)\) consists only of rotations and reflections. Every reflection is of finite order with \(n = 2\). The rotation \(\mathcal{A}_\theta\) for \(\theta \in (0, -\pi, \pi]\) is of finite order if and only if there exist \(n, m \in mathbb{R}\) such that \(n \cdot \theta = m \cdot 2 \pi\) is a multiple of \(2\pi\), which is equivalent to saying \(\theta \in \pi \mathbb{Q}\).
