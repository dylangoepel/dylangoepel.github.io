Solving \(f'' = f' + f\)

Let \(f \in \mathcal{C}^\infty(\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{R})\) with

\[ f'' = f' + f. \]

To solve this equation, we observe that because of

\[ f'' = F_2 f' + F_1 f \]

and

\[\begin{aligned} f^{(n + 1)} &= (F_n f' + F_{n - 1} f)' \\ &= F_n f'' + F_{n - 1} f' \\ &= (F_n + F_{n - 1}) f' + F_n f \\ &= F_{n + 1} f' + F_n f \end{aligned}\]

where \( (F_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \) denotes the Fibonacci-sequence, by induction, we get

\[ f^{(n)} = F_n f' + F_{n - 1} f \]

for all \(n \in \mathbb{N}\). As \(F_n \in o(n!)\), we can conclude that \(Tf = f\). Let \(x \in \mathbb{R}\). Using the Moivre-Binet-formula, we get

\[\begin{aligned} f(x) &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{f^{(n)}(0)}{n!} x^n \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{F_n f'(0) + F_{n - 1} f(0)}{n!} x^n \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{(\phi^n - \psi^n) f'(0) + (\phi^{n - 1} - \psi^{n - 1}) f(0)}{\sqrt{5} n!} x^n \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{\phi^n (f'(0) + \phi^{-1} f(0)) - \psi^n (f'(0) + \psi^{-1} f(0))}{\sqrt{5} n!} x^n \\ &= \frac{f'(0) + \phi^{-1} f(0)}{\sqrt{5}} e^{\phi x} - \frac{f'(0) + \psi^{-1} f(0)}{\sqrt{5}} e^{\psi x}. \end{aligned}\]

As we can freely choose \(f'(0), f(0) \in \mathbb{R}\), the solution set for our equation equals

\[ L = \langle e^{\phi x}, e^{\psi x} \rangle, \]

where

\[ \phi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \]

and

\[ \psi = \frac{1 - \sqrt{5}}{2}. \]
